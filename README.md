## Understanding Sports Betting Types in ChatsAndBetz

  

Before diving into the pricing and community details, let me explain the types of bets you'll encounter in this ChatsAndBetz sports betting community.

  

### Moneyline Bets

  

These are the simplest bets where you pick which team will win outright. No point spreads or handicaps involved.

  

The ChatsAndBetz cappers often use moneyline bets when they're confident about a clear favorite or see value in an underdog.

  

### Point Spread Betting

  

This is the most common type in American sports betting. The favorite "gives" points while the underdog "takes" points.

  

For example, if the Lakers are -4.5 against the Warriors, they need to win by 5 or more points for your bet to cash. The ChatsAndBetz picks often focus heavily on spread betting since it levels the playing field.

  

### Over/Under Totals

  

These bets focus on the combined final score of both teams. You're betting whether the total will be over or under the sportsbook's posted number.

  

The community's cappers excel at totals betting, especially in sports like NBA and NFL where they can analyze pace of play and team tendencies.

  

### Parlay Betting

  

Parlays combine multiple bets for a higher payout, but all selections must win. The ChatsAndBetz community sometimes offers parlay suggestions, though they emphasize that these are riskier plays.

  

In the USA, parlays account for upward of 70% of each state's total sportsbook revenue each month, so the community teaches proper bankroll management with these bets.

  

### Live Betting

  

This is where ChatsAndBetz really shines. Live betting allows you to place wagers while games are in progress, and the community's real-time alerts can be incredibly valuable.

  

Live betting has changed sports betting mechanics by turning it from a discontinuous form of gambling into a continuous one. The community helps members navigate this fast-paced environment.

  

### Proposition Bets

  

These are wagers on specific outcomes not related to the final score. Think player props like "Will LeBron James score over 25.5 points?"

  

The ChatsAndBetz cappers often identify value in proposition bets where their research gives them an edge over the general betting public.

  

### Futures Betting

  

These are long-term wagers like "Which team will win the Super Bowl?" The community occasionally shares futures plays, especially early in seasons when they spot value.

  

### Teaser Bets

  

Teasers give you better point spreads or totals in exchange for lower payouts. The ChatsAndBetz community uses these strategically when they like multiple games but want extra insurance.

## Sports Betting Legality and Compliance

  

Before we go further, I need to address the legal landscape of sports betting in the United States.

  

### Current Legal Status

  

Sports betting was previously illegal under the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act of 1992 (PASPA). However, the Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018.

  

This opened the door for individual states to legalize sports betting. Since that decision, over $20 billion has been spent on sports betting in the United States as of May 2020.

  

### State-by-State Breakdown

  

As of January 2024:

-   **37 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** have operational legalized sports betting
-   **North Carolina** has legalized it but operations will begin later in 2024
-   **30 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** have legalized online sports betting
-   **8 states** only allow betting through physical sportsbooks
